## About

I've been drawn to computers for as long as I can remember, but the real turning point was a school computer class where our teacher showed us you could build a website with just Notepad. That was it — the idea that a blank text file could become something you could see and interact with completely hooked me.

The problems that genuinely excite me are the ones where a good solution makes something meaningfully easier, faster, or more accessible for people. If building it moves something forward — for a user, a team, or an industry — I'm in, headfirst.

I tend to spend more time upfront locking in architecture and core decisions, because getting that right is what lets me move fast later. But I'm just as particular about how something feels to use. Something that works but feels wrong to interact with isn't really finished to me.

I'm building toward roles where product thinking and AI engineering overlap — specifically, work where "should we add AI?" is the wrong question entirely, and the real work is designing intelligence in from the start.
